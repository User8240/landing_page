About Me ✎

Grace Kostanich

Programming has always seemed to me like it was out of my reach. I'd always been awful at learning languages, so I couldn't imagine myself learning to code. It wasn't until I took my first computer science class in highschool that I had much interest in it; asside from thinking it was cool and some form of hacking. After joining the class, I fell in love with HTML. Although I may struggle with languages other than english (english too, sometimes), I'd been an avid journaler since I turned 14. I loved writing, and HTML reminded me of Google Docs or Word. It felt comfortable to me, almost like I already knew the language; or perhaps the grammar.

At the start of the semester we learned how to convert numbers into binary, and then into hexadecimal. I was fairly good at math at the time, and this made me feel like I was even better. I had never applied my creativity in this way, and my math skills were paying off simultaneously! I'd never felt like I was a very smart student before, but this class showed me what I truly could do. The teacher and I worked together on many things. I really looked up to him, and deep down, I felt grateful to him. I wasn't aware of all he was doing for me at the time, but the progress I made that year was very clear. I was able to bring my gpa up to a 3.9 that year for the first time in my life. The confidence I gained from this one class changed the way I view learning. I started to see that I could go places in my life, maybe even be successful and pay my mom back for all she's done for me. I could make my dad and step-Mom proud of me.

Coding taught me that I do not have to be good at everything to be confident. I don't have to over-achieve to gain anyones respect. I learned that I was deserving of these things regardless of how intelligent or capable others may percieve me to be.

I joined Epicodus not only because I value the school and it's accessibility/ affordability, but because I'm passionate about coding and so is this school. The same way a writer is passionate about their article, paper or journal. One day I will be very proud to say I was a student at Epicodus.
